Minseok (Mason) Jung is a PhD student in Informatics at Indiana University Bloomington, specializing in Complex Systems and Network Science. His research focuses on using and developing advanced AI and data science techniques to understand innovation in science and technology. He is also interested in exploring how large language models can be enhanced and understood through the lens of collective intelligence.

Mason is currently a Systems Engineering Fellow at the App Inventor Foundation and conducts research at MIT CSAIL. Before joining the PhD program, he completed his undergraduate studies in Physics at Pohang University of Science and Technology (POSTECH).

Research Interests

Mason's research spans several interconnected areas in artificial intelligence and computational social science:

  • AI Systems - Development and analysis of artificial intelligence systems and their societal impact
  • Information Technology Policy - Intersection of technology development and policy frameworks
  • Natural Language Processing - Language models and their applications in social contexts
  • Social Computing - Computational approaches to understanding social phenomena
  • Complex Systems and Network Science - Mathematical modeling of complex social and technological systems
  • Science of Science - Quantitative approaches to understanding scientific innovation and discovery

Research Projects

Mason is currently conducting research on topic modeling to identify thematic trends and curricular variations in K-12 computer science education documents across the United States. His work involves developing computational methods to analyze educational content and policy documents.

His doctoral research focuses on understanding innovation patterns in science and technology through the application of advanced data science techniques and network analysis methods.
